01 / What The System Does

A market pricing intelligence engine tracking competitor prices, product changes, and customer sentiment across your entire market

The market pricing pipeline monitors your competitive landscape across four dimensions. The price layer tracks competitor pricing across every configured channel — websites, marketplaces, distributor catalogs, and government contract vehicles. The product layer monitors feature additions, removals, and modifications. The sentiment layer analyzes customer reviews, NPS data, and social media discussion. The market layer tracks market share estimates, channel mix shifts, and new entrant activity.

The system monitors 10,000+ SKUs or service line items per cycle. For each product or service in your portfolio, it maintains a competitive positioning map: your price vs. every tracked competitor, feature parity analysis, customer sentiment comparison, and recommended pricing actions. When a competitor changes their pricing or launches a new product, you know within hours — not weeks.

03 / What You Get

A weekly pricing brief with competitive moves, sentiment shifts, and pricing recommendations

Weekly Brief

Delivered every Monday. Contains: competitor price changes with impact analysis, new product or feature launches from competitors, customer sentiment trends by product and feature, market share movement indicators, and pricing optimization recommendations with elasticity estimates.

Alert Types

  • Price Change — Tracked competitor changes pricing. Direction, magnitude, and recommended response with elasticity analysis.
  • Product Launch — Competitor launches new product or major feature. Competitive positioning impact and response options.
  • Sentiment Shift — Customer sentiment changes significantly for your product or a competitor. Root cause analysis.
  • Market Entry — New competitor enters your market. Profile, pricing strategy, and recommended defensive actions.
  • Margin Alert — Market pricing trends threaten your margins. Optimization recommendations with scenario modeling.
04 / Use Cases

Specific scenarios where the system changes the outcome

Scenario 01

A SaaS company needs to understand why they keep losing deals on price

The system reveals that the primary competitor offers a lower-tier plan at 40% less that captures evaluation-stage buyers, then upsells. Feature comparison shows the competitor's base plan matches 70% of the SaaS company's starter tier. Recommendation: introduce a comparable entry plan. Win rate on competitive deals improves 22%.

Scenario 02

A manufacturer needs to optimize pricing across 2,000 SKUs and 4 channels

The system monitors competitor pricing for comparable products across retail, distribution, e-commerce, and government channels. It identifies 340 SKUs priced 15%+ above market without corresponding feature differentiation and 180 SKUs priced 20%+ below market with room for margin improvement. Implementing the recommendations increases gross margin by $4.2M annually.

Scenario 03

A government contractor needs to maintain competitive GSA Schedule pricing

The system tracks FPDS award data for competitors on the same SINs. It identifies where competitors are winning based on price and where the contractor's rates are above the competitive median. Quarterly pricing reports enable targeted rate adjustments that improve task order win rates from 18% to 31%.
