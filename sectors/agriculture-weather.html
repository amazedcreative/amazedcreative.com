01 / What The System Does

An agricultural intelligence engine combining weather, crop reports, and commodity markets into actionable decisions

The agriculture pipeline fuses five data layers. The weather layer processes NOAA forecasts, satellite imagery, radar data, and historical patterns. The crop layer monitors USDA progress reports, condition ratings, acreage data, and yield estimates. The market layer tracks commodity futures, basis levels, export inspections, and ethanol data. The soil layer processes moisture, temperature, and nutrient data from USDA-NRCS. The logistics layer monitors elevator bids, transportation rates, and export terminal capacity.

For each configured region and commodity, the system generates a decision matrix: optimal planting windows, pricing signals for pre-harvest contracting, storage vs. sell decisions, and crop insurance trigger analysis. The system runs 90-day forward models combining weather probability with market dynamics to generate price range projections.

03 / What You Get

A weekly agricultural brief with weather outlook, market analysis, and action recommendations

Weekly Brief

Delivered every Monday. Contains: 14-day and 90-day weather outlooks, crop condition vs. historical averages, commodity price analysis with basis trends, input cost tracking, export demand signals, and specific action recommendations (plant, spray, harvest, sell, store).

Alert Types

  • Weather Event — Significant weather approaching growing region. Crop impact assessment and protective action recommendations.
  • Market Signal — Commodity price breaks key level. Position management recommendations with rationale.
  • Crop Condition — Material change in crop conditions for a key region. Supply impact and price implications.
  • Basis Opportunity — Local basis narrows to historically attractive level. Sell timing recommendation.
  • Policy Change — USDA program rule change affecting your operation. Compliance timeline and financial impact.
