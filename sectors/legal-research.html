01 / What The System Does

An autonomous legal research engine that finds relevant precedents, tracks case developments, and drafts summaries

The legal research pipeline automates the most time-consuming aspects of legal work. The case law layer scans federal and state court opinions, identifying relevant precedents by jurisdiction, topic, and legal principle. The filings layer monitors new complaints, motions, and orders across tracked jurisdictions and case types. The regulatory layer scans agency enforcement actions, consent decrees, and administrative law judge decisions. The analysis layer uses AI to draft case summaries, compare holdings across jurisdictions, and identify circuit splits.

For each configured legal topic or matter, the system generates a comprehensive research package: relevant precedents ranked by authority and recency, case development timeline, opposing counsel's argument patterns from prior filings, judge-specific ruling tendencies, and drafted memorandum sections. The system reduces a 20-hour research assignment to a 3-hour review and refinement task.

03 / What You Get

A weekly legal intelligence brief with case developments, regulatory changes, and research updates

Weekly Brief

Delivered every Monday. Contains: new opinions in tracked practice areas with holding summaries, case development updates on tracked matters, regulatory action summaries with compliance implications, legislative activity affecting your practice areas, and circuit split tracking.

Alert Types

  • New Precedent — Significant opinion issued in your practice area. Holding summary, circuit implications, and impact analysis.
  • Case Development — Tracked case receives significant ruling. Impact on your matters and strategy implications.
  • Regulatory Action — Agency enforcement action in your area. Compliance implications and recommended client alerts.
  • Legislative Change — Bill advancing that affects your practice area. Timeline, probability assessment, and client impact.
  • Circuit Split — Conflicting holdings identified across circuits. Supreme Court cert potential and implications.
04 / Use Cases

Specific scenarios where the system changes the outcome

Scenario 01

A litigation firm needs to research every case a specific judge has ruled on in patent disputes

The system pulls every opinion, order, and memorandum from the judge over 10 years. It analyzes claim construction tendencies, Markman hearing patterns, summary judgment rates, and damages methodology preferences. The litigation team adjusts their strategy based on this judge's specific patterns.

Scenario 02

A regulatory compliance team needs to track every FDA enforcement action in their product category

The system monitors warning letters, consent decrees, import alerts, and Form 483 observations. When enforcement patterns shift, the system alerts with trend analysis and recommended compliance adjustments. The team addresses issues before the next inspection.

Scenario 03

A corporate law department needs to monitor litigation trends affecting their industry

The system tracks new filings by case type, plaintiff firm, and legal theory across all federal courts. When a new wave of class action filings begins targeting their industry, the system alerts with plaintiff strategy analysis and defensive recommendations 60 days before they would normally learn about the trend.
