01 / What The System Does

Maps the $886 billion defense budget into actionable intelligence for contractors and analysts

The defense intelligence pipeline operates across three layers. The first layer scans all DoD procurement activity — contract awards, modifications, solicitations, and cancellations across every military service branch and defense agency. The second layer correlates spending data with congressional appropriations, DOGE efficiency reviews, and force structure announcements to identify where money is moving. The third layer builds predictive models: which programs are expanding, which are being consolidated, and where the recompete windows are opening.

The system tracks 47 defense agencies and sub-agencies independently. It maps prime-sub relationships across the top 200 defense contractors and monitors teaming arrangements, mentor-protege agreements, and joint venture formations. When DOGE cancels a contract, the system immediately identifies the replacement requirement timeline and flags it for pursuit.

Contracts

DoD Contract Announcements

Daily contract award announcements from defense.gov. Every award over $7.5M with contractor, location, work description, and contracting command.

Budget

DoD Budget Justification Books

R-1, P-1, O-1 budget exhibits. Program-level funding by fiscal year. Research, procurement, and O&M line items mapped to contracts.

FPDS Defense Filters

All DoD award data filtered by service branch, contracting command, NAICS, PSC, and contract type. Historical pricing and period of performance.

Industry

03 / What You Get

A weekly defense brief with spending shifts, contract movements, and positioning windows

Weekly Defense Intelligence Brief

Delivered every Monday. Contains: top contract awards of the week with analysis, DOGE impact tracker (new cancellations and recompete windows), budget movement signals from congressional activity, competitor teaming and hiring intelligence, and a 90-day opportunity pipeline ranked by win probability.

Alert Types

  • DOGE Cancellation — Contract terminated or reduced. Includes affected contractor, program, and estimated recompete timeline.
  • Budget Shift — Reprogramming action or continuing resolution impact on specific defense programs.
  • Prime-Sub Change — Major teaming arrangement formation or dissolution in your market segment.
  • Force Structure Signal — Unit activation, deactivation, or relocation creating new service requirements.
  • Classified-to-Unclass Transition — Previously classified program appearing in unclassified procurement for the first time.
04 / Use Cases

How defense contractors use the intelligence system

Scenario 01

A mid-tier defense contractor tracks DOGE cancellations for recompete positioning

DOGE cancels 340 IT modernization contracts across DoD. The system identifies 47 that match the contractor's capabilities. For each, it maps the incumbent, remaining period of performance, contracting officer, and estimated replacement timeline. The contractor captures three displaced requirements worth $28M in the first quarter — opportunities they would have missed without automated tracking.

Scenario 02

An intelligence analyst tracks spending concentration in Space Force programs

The system maps all USSF contract obligations by contractor and program. It identifies that 72% of Space Force IT spending flows through four contractors. When the FY27 budget adds a new space domain awareness program, the system flags it as a market entry opportunity and identifies the likely vehicle (ASTRO) and competitive landscape.

Scenario 03

A small business monitors mentor-protege opportunities with defense primes

The entity graph tracks every mentor-protege agreement filed with DoD OSBP. The system identifies which primes are actively seeking proteges in specific NAICS codes, which agreements are expiring, and which primes have subcontracting plan shortfalls. The small business receives targeted outreach recommendations with specific past performance alignment.

Scenario 04

A defense consultancy needs early warning on base realignment impacts

The system monitors congressional BRAC-related language, military construction appropriations, and unit movement orders. When signals align — reduced MILCON at a base, unit PCS orders, and local contract wind-downs — the system generates a realignment probability assessment with a timeline and impact on existing contracts in the area.
