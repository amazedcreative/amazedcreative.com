01 / What The System Does

An insurance claims intelligence engine that detects fraud, accelerates processing, and identifies patterns across your entire book

The insurance claims pipeline processes claims data through four analytical layers. The intake layer classifies incoming claims by type, complexity, and urgency. The pattern layer compares each claim against historical patterns — provider billing norms, claimant behavior profiles, regional claim frequencies, and seasonal baselines. The anomaly layer flags statistical outliers: claims that deviate from expected patterns in timing, amount, provider combination, or documentation quality. The investigation layer generates SIU referral packages for flagged claims with supporting evidence and comparable fraud case references.

The system processes thousands of claims per day. For each claim, it generates a risk score (0-100) based on 40+ fraud indicators. Claims scoring above threshold are automatically routed to SIU with a pre-built investigation package. The system also identifies systemic patterns — provider rings, staged accident networks, and premium fraud schemes — that individual claim review would miss. Average fraud detection improvement: 35-50% increase in SIU referral accuracy.

03 / What You Get

A weekly claims intelligence brief with fraud patterns, processing metrics, and investigation priorities

Weekly Brief

Delivered every Monday. Contains: flagged claims requiring SIU review with risk scores and evidence packages, emerging fraud pattern alerts, processing efficiency metrics and bottleneck identification, reserve adequacy analysis, and litigation trend tracking by jurisdiction.

Alert Types

  • Fraud Flag — Claim scores above fraud threshold. Evidence package with comparable cases and investigation recommendations.
  • Pattern Alert — New fraud pattern detected across multiple claims. Network analysis and affected policy identification.
  • Reserve Warning — Claim development exceeding reserves. Recommended adjustment with supporting loss development data.
  • Provider Anomaly — Healthcare provider billing pattern deviates from norms. Utilization analysis and referral recommendation.
  • Litigation Signal — Attorney representation patterns shifting in a jurisdiction. Defense strategy adjustments recommended.
04 / Use Cases

Specific scenarios where the system changes the outcome

Scenario 01

A property insurer needs to detect organized fraud rings after a hurricane

The system analyzes claims clustering by geography, timing, contractor, and public adjuster. When multiple claims from different policyholders use the same contractor, public adjuster, and attorney — and all were filed within 48 hours of each other — the system identifies the ring and generates an SIU package. Detection rate for organized fraud improves 60%.

Scenario 02

A health insurer needs to identify upcoding and unbundling patterns

The system compares provider billing patterns against specialty-specific baselines. When a provider consistently bills at the highest complexity level or unbundles procedures that should be billed as a single code, the system flags the pattern with statistical evidence. Recovery on identified overpayments: $8M in the first year.

Scenario 03

An auto insurer needs to reduce claims cycle time while maintaining accuracy

The system auto-classifies claims by complexity and routes them to the appropriate adjuster skill level. Simple claims (30% of volume) are fast-tracked with automated damage estimates. Complex claims receive enhanced investigation packages. Average cycle time decreases 25% while accuracy improves.
