01 / What The System Does

From RFP release to draft response in hours instead of weeks

The RFP automation pipeline begins the moment a solicitation is published. The system downloads the complete solicitation package — statement of work, instructions to offerors, evaluation criteria, CDRLs, and all attachments. It extracts every discrete requirement and maps them into a structured requirements matrix. Each requirement is tagged by section, type (mandatory/desired), evaluation factor, and compliance difficulty.

Stage two pulls from your company's knowledge base: past performance narratives, technical approaches, resumes, certifications, and corporate capability data. The system matches past performance to current requirements and identifies gaps. Stage three generates the compliance matrix — requirement by requirement, with your proposed response approach and reference to supporting evidence. Stage four drafts proposal sections: technical approach, management plan, staffing plan, and past performance volumes. Each section is structured to the solicitation's instructions and evaluation criteria.

The output is a first draft, not a final submission. But it compresses a 40-hour first-draft cycle into 4-6 hours of system processing, giving your proposal team a 3-week head start on refinement.

02 / Data Sources

The inputs the system processes for every proposal

Solicitation

RFP Document Package

Full solicitation including SOW, PWS, instructions, evaluation criteria, CDRLs, clauses, and all amendments. PDF extraction with structure preservation.

Corporate

Past Performance Library

Your company's completed contracts, CPARS ratings, performance narratives, and reference contacts. Indexed by NAICS, agency, and capability.

Personnel

Resume Database

Key personnel resumes, certifications, clearances, and availability status. Skills matrix for matching to labor category requirements.

Technical

Solution Architecture Library

Previous technical approaches, architecture diagrams, methodology descriptions, and tool sets. Reusable components indexed by capability area.

Pricing

Historical Rate Data

Your loaded labor rates, subcontractor rates, ODC estimates, and historical pricing from winning proposals. Rate comparison against FPDS data.

Competitor

Entity Graph Data

Incumbent analysis, competitor pricing benchmarks, teaming landscape, and evaluation trend data from the competitive analysis system.

Q&A

Industry Day / Q&A Data

Questions and answers from industry days, pre-proposal conferences, and amendment Q&A logs. Interpretation guidance extracted.

Compliance

FAR/DFARS Clause Library

Full clause text and compliance requirement extraction. Auto-identifies applicable clauses and required certifications per solicitation.

Templates

Proposal Templates

Section templates by proposal type (LPTA, best value, trade-off). Formatting requirements, page limits, and font specifications.

03 / What You Get

A complete proposal framework ready for your team to refine

Proposal Output Package

Delivered within 4-6 hours of RFP ingestion. Contains: structured compliance matrix mapping every requirement to a response approach, draft technical volume with approach narrative per evaluation factor, draft management volume with staffing plan and org chart, past performance volume with matched references and narratives, pricing framework with rate recommendations based on competitive data, and a gap analysis identifying requirements where your company needs additional support.

Alert Types

  • New RFP Match — Solicitation published matching your bid criteria. Full analysis and bid/no-bid recommendation within 2 hours.

  • Competitor Signal — Intelligence suggesting a competitor is pursuing the same opportunity. Pricing and teaming implications analyzed.
04 / Use Cases

How the system changes proposal operations

Scenario 01

A small business needs to respond to 3 RFPs simultaneously with a 2-person proposal team

Each RFP has 200+ requirements and a 30-day response window. The system extracts requirements from all three solicitations, generates compliance matrices, and drafts initial volumes for each within 24 hours. The proposal team focuses on refinement and win strategy instead of document assembly. All three proposals submit on time with full compliance — previously impossible without a 6-person team.

Scenario 02

A mid-tier contractor needs to generate a bid/no-bid analysis within 48 hours of RFP release

The system ingests the RFP, maps requirements to company capabilities, identifies past performance alignment, analyzes the competitive landscape (incumbent, known competitors, pricing benchmarks), and generates a scored bid/no-bid recommendation. Includes specific risk factors, estimated proposal cost, win probability, and recommended teaming partners if needed.

Scenario 03

A large contractor needs to maintain consistency across 40+ active proposals

The system maintains a centralized knowledge base of approved technical approaches, past performance narratives, and boilerplate content. When a proposal team drafts a section, the system checks it against the approved library for consistency, flags deviations, and suggests standardized language. Quality control at scale without a dedicated quality team reviewing every page.

Scenario 04

A consulting firm needs to generate tailored capability statements for every opportunity

For each new solicitation, the system extracts the agency's priorities, maps them to the firm's capabilities, pulls the most relevant past performance references, and generates a 2-page capability statement customized to that specific opportunity. Turnaround: 20 minutes per statement instead of 3 hours of manual work.
