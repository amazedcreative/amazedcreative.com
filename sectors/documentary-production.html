01 / What The System Does

Turns data investigations into production-ready documentary briefs

The documentary production pipeline bridges the gap between intelligence findings and visual storytelling. When the intelligence engine surfaces a compelling finding — spending anomalies, concentration patterns, policy impacts, industry shifts — the production system takes over. It structures the finding into a three-act narrative arc. Act one establishes the world and the stakes. Act two presents the investigation, the data, the characters involved. Act three delivers the implications and what happens next.

For each act, the system generates: a scene-by-scene breakdown with suggested visual treatments, a list of b-roll requirements with specific shot descriptions, data visualization specifications for key statistics, interview subject recommendations with suggested questions, sound design direction including mood, tempo, and silence beats, and a voiceover script outline with key lines and transitions. The system also generates AI music generation cues — BPM, key, instrumentation, and emotional arc — that can be fed directly into music generation tools.

A documentary brief that would take a research team 2-3 weeks to assemble arrives in 4-6 hours. The creative team focuses on craft — shooting, editing, grading — instead of research.

04 / Use Cases

How production teams use automated research to tell better stories faster

Scenario 01

The intelligence engine finds that one contractor controls 84% of a federal agency's IT budget

The production system generates a 12-page brief: cold open with the dollar figure and a single question, act one establishing the agency's mission and IT dependency, act two walking through the data trail — 14 connected programs, zero competitive awards, entity graph showing the relationship network, act three exploring what it means for taxpayers and national security. B-roll list includes agency headquarters, congressional hearing footage references, and data visualization mockups. Sound design calls for tension build through act two with a silence beat before the 84% reveal.

Scenario 02

A YouTube channel needs weekly content from government spending investigations

The system generates one production brief per week from the intelligence engine's top finding. Each brief is formatted for 12-18 minute YouTube format: hook in the first 15 seconds, data-driven narrative through the middle, and a cliffhanger or call-to-action close. Thumbnail concepts and title options included. The channel produces consistent weekly content without a dedicated research team.

Scenario 03

A news organization needs to turn a FOIA response into a broadcast segment

The system ingests the FOIA documents, extracts key findings, cross-references with public spending data and entity relationships, and generates a broadcast-ready brief: 3-minute segment structure, key quotes from documents, data points for graphics, and suggested interview angles. Research that would take an investigative team days is structured and ready for the producer in hours.

Scenario 04

A podcast producer needs deep-dive episode outlines on defense spending topics

The system generates a 60-minute episode outline: topic introduction, data presentation segments, expert interview preparation (with background on each guest and specific questions), listener engagement points, and episode summary. Each outline includes timestamped segment breaks, transition cues, and recommended follow-up topics for future episodes. Production prep time drops from 20 hours to 3.
