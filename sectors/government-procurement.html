01 / What The System Does

An autonomous pipeline that replaces the 40-hour-per-week task of monitoring government opportunities

The system runs a five-stage agent pipeline every 24 hours. Stage one scans every configured procurement portal and ingests new solicitations, modifications, award notices, and pre-solicitation notices. Stage two classifies each record by NAICS code, set-aside type, contract vehicle, and agency. Stage three scores each opportunity against your company profile — past performance, certifications, geographic reach, and contract ceiling preferences. Stage four enriches high-scoring matches with competitor analysis, incumbent research, and pricing benchmarks from historical award data. Stage five delivers a prioritized daily brief to your team with recommended actions.

The entity graph tracks relationships between contractors, contracting officers, agencies, and contract vehicles across 2.1 million historical awards. When a new opportunity appears, the system already knows who won the last iteration, what they charged, and who their teaming partners were.

02 / Data Sources

Every public procurement data source the federal government maintains

Primary Portal

SAM.gov

Active solicitations, modifications, award notices, pre-solicitation notices, sources sought, and special notices. Full-text search with NAICS and PSC filtering.

Award Data

FPDS-NG

Federal Procurement Data System. Every contract award, modification, and closeout since 2004. Pricing, period of performance, competition type, set-aside status.

Spending Data

USASpending.gov

Obligation-level spending data tied to awards, agencies, and recipients. Budget function mapping, congressional district tracking, sub-award data.

Grants

Grants.gov

Federal grant opportunities with eligibility requirements, funding amounts, and application deadlines. Cross-referenced with agency spending patterns.

Forecast

Agency Procurement Forecasts

Published procurement forecasts from DoD, DHS, HHS, VA, and civilian agencies. Pre-RFP signals for upcoming requirements.

Vehicles

GSA Advantage / eBuy

GSA Schedule RFQs, BPAs, and task order opportunities. Schedule holder pricing comparisons and sales volume data.

Sub-Contract

SBA SubNet

Subcontracting opportunities posted by prime contractors. Matched to your NAICS codes and small business certifications.

State & Local

State Procurement Portals

50-state portal scanning covering BidNet, PublicPurchase, IonWave, Bonfire, and proprietary state systems. Configurable by state.

Intelligence

Federal Register

Proposed rules, final rules, and notices that signal upcoming procurement changes. Agency reorganizations, program terminations, and new requirements.

03 / What You Get

A daily intelligence brief and real-time alerts when high-value opportunities appear

Weekly Procurement Brief

Delivered every Monday at 0700 EST. Contains: new opportunities matching your profile (scored and ranked), competitor win/loss activity from the previous week, upcoming deadlines within 30 days, pricing intelligence on awards in your NAICS codes, and forecast alerts for requirements in the 90-day pipeline.

Alert Types

  • Opportunity Match — New solicitation scores above your threshold. Includes incumbent, estimated value, and competition analysis.
  • Competitor Win — A tracked competitor receives an award in your market. Pricing and teaming details included.
  • Modification Alert — A tracked solicitation receives an amendment. Scope change, deadline extension, or Q&A update.
  • Forecast Signal — Agency publishes a procurement forecast entry matching your capabilities. Pre-RFP positioning window.
  • Pricing Anomaly — Award pricing deviates significantly from historical norms. Potential protest opportunity or market shift.
  • Set-Aside Change — Solicitation set-aside status changes. Full-and-open to small business, or vice versa.
04 / Use Cases

Specific scenarios where the system changes the outcome

Scenario 01

A cybersecurity firm needs to find every DoD opportunity matching their CMMC Level 2 certification

The system scans SAM.gov, eBuy, and DoD-specific portals daily. It filters by NAICS 541512, 541519, and 518210. It cross-references each opportunity against the firm's past performance database and scores win probability based on incumbent analysis, contract ceiling, and set-aside status. The firm receives 12-15 qualified opportunities per week instead of the 2-3 they were finding manually.

Scenario 02

A small business wants to identify subcontracting opportunities with large prime contractors

The entity graph maps teaming relationships across 50,000+ prime contractors. When a large IDIQ award posts, the system identifies which primes won task orders, what small business subcontracting plans they filed, and which NAICS codes they need partners for. The system generates a teaming outreach list with contact information and shared past performance references.

Scenario 03

An IT services company needs to track when incumbents lose recompetes

The system monitors all contract expirations within the company's NAICS codes. When an incumbent loses a recompete or a contract is cancelled (including DOGE-related terminations), the system alerts with the replacement timeline, contracting officer, and new vehicle status. The company gets a 60-90 day head start on positioning for the follow-on.

Scenario 04

A construction firm tracking federal facility projects across five states

The system scans Army Corps of Engineers, GSA PBS, VA CFMO, and state DOT portals. It filters by project type, estimated value, and geographic region. Each match includes historical pricing data for similar projects, known competitors in the region, and Davis-Bacon wage determination references. Weekly brief includes a 90-day project forecast.

Scenario 05

A consulting firm needs competitive pricing intelligence for their GSA Schedule

The system pulls pricing data from GSA Advantage, FPDS award history, and eBuy RFQ responses. It benchmarks the firm's rates against competitors on the same SINs. Quarterly pricing reports show market trends, rate compression patterns, and recommended adjustments for the next modification.
