01 / What The System Does

Builds a living entity graph of your competitive landscape and updates it daily

The competitive analysis pipeline starts by defining your market — NAICS codes, contract vehicles, agencies, and geographies. Then it builds a comprehensive graph of every entity operating in that space. Contractors, contracting officers, agencies, vehicles, awards, modifications, and teaming relationships are all nodes in the graph. The system updates this graph daily with new award data, solicitation activity, and public filings.

The analysis engine runs five investigation types against the graph every cycle. Win/loss analysis tracks which competitors are gaining or losing market share. Pricing analysis benchmarks award values against historical norms. Teaming analysis maps who works with whom and identifies shifting alliances. Hiring analysis monitors competitor job postings for capability expansion signals. Concentration analysis identifies monopoly positions and market vulnerabilities.

Every week, the system generates a competitive landscape report with entity-level detail: who won what, at what price, using which vehicle, and with which teaming partners. Changes from the previous period are highlighted automatically.

02 / Data Sources

The data feeds that power the entity graph

Awards

FPDS-NG

Complete federal award history. Contractor, value, competition type, set-aside, vehicle, NAICS, PSC, contracting agency, and period of performance.

Spending

USASpending.gov

Obligation-level data with sub-award visibility. Prime-sub relationships, geographic distribution, and fiscal year trends.

Entities

SAM.gov Entity Data

Contractor registrations including CAGE codes, DUNS/UEI, certifications, socioeconomic categories, and POC information.

Solicitations

SAM.gov Opportunities

Active and archived solicitations. Tracks who is bidding on what by monitoring Q&A submissions and amendment acknowledgments.

Hiring

LinkedIn / Job Boards

Competitor job postings analyzed for capability expansion, new market entry signals, and key personnel movements.

Financial

SEC Filings / Earnings

Public company financials, revenue segments, backlog data, and earnings call transcripts for competitor financial health.

Partnerships

Mentor-Protege / JV Filings

SBA mentor-protege agreements, joint venture formations, and teaming arrangement announcements.

Legal

GAO Protest Decisions

Bid protest filings and decisions. Shows competitor pricing disputes, evaluation challenges, and procurement process insights.

News

Industry Press / PR

Press releases, award announcements, merger news, and executive appointments across competitor organizations.

04 / Use Cases

How organizations use competitive intelligence to win

Scenario 01

A federal IT company needs to understand why they keep losing to the same competitor

The entity graph reveals the competitor has won 14 awards on the same GWAC in the past 18 months, all with the same contracting officer at DHS. Pricing analysis shows they consistently bid 12-15% below market rate. The system identifies that the competitor recently formed a JV with a service-disabled veteran-owned firm, gaining set-aside access. The client adjusts their strategy: different vehicle, different contracting command, competitive pricing.

Scenario 02

A defense contractor tracks competitor hiring to predict market moves

The system detects that three competitors have posted 40+ cybersecurity roles with CMMC assessor qualifications in the past 60 days. Cross-referenced with DoD's CMMC rollout timeline, this signals a surge in CMMC assessment demand. The client accelerates their own hiring and captures early assessment contracts before the market saturates.

Scenario 03

A private equity firm evaluates a government contractor acquisition target

The system generates a complete competitive profile: the target's win rate, average contract value, vehicle access, teaming relationships, customer concentration, and market share trend over five years. It identifies that 68% of revenue comes from a single agency and that three competitors are positioned to take share in the next recompete cycle. The PE firm adjusts their valuation accordingly.

Scenario 04

A management consulting firm needs to identify teaming partners for a large DoD pursuit

The entity graph queries for firms with complementary capabilities (cloud migration, zero trust architecture), active DoD past performance, and no existing teaming arrangement with the primary competitor. The system returns 12 potential partners ranked by capability alignment, past performance relevance, and small business certification value. Draft teaming outreach emails included.
