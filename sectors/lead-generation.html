01 / What The System Does

A 24/7 business development engine that finds, scores, and enriches leads while your team sleeps

The lead generation pipeline operates in four stages. Stage one scans configured data sources — procurement portals, industry databases, company directories, job postings, funding announcements, and news feeds. Stage two classifies each lead against your ideal customer profile: industry, size, geography, technology stack, budget signals, and timing indicators. Stage three scores every classified lead on a 0-100 scale using weighted criteria you define. Stage four enriches the top-scoring leads with AI-generated analysis: company overview, decision-maker identification, pain point mapping, and recommended outreach approach.

Built for a cybersecurity company, the system scans 25,000+ potential leads per pipeline run. After classification and scoring, approximately 200-400 qualified leads surface per week. Each arrives with a complete dossier: company profile, technology environment signals, recent funding or contract activity, identified decision-makers, and a draft outreach message tailored to their specific situation.

03 / What You Get

A scored, enriched pipeline delivered to your CRM every morning

Daily Lead Brief

Delivered at 0700 EST. Contains: new qualified leads scored above threshold (sorted by score), trigger event summary for each lead, decision-maker identification with LinkedIn profiles, recommended outreach approach and draft messaging, and CRM-ready export with all enrichment fields populated.

Alert Types

  • High-Score Lead — Lead scores 85+ on your criteria. Full enrichment package with recommended same-day outreach.
  • Trigger Event — A tracked company experiences a buying signal: funding round, leadership change, breach, or expansion.
  • Competitor Displacement — A company's contract with your competitor expires or they post roles indicating a technology change.
  • Pipeline Decay — Leads in your existing pipeline show disengagement signals. Re-engagement recommendations included.
  • Market Surge — Unusual spike in leads from a specific industry or geography. Market opportunity analysis attached.
04 / Use Cases

Specific scenarios where automated lead gen outperforms manual prospecting

Scenario 01

A cybersecurity firm needs to fill their pipeline with CMMC-ready companies

The system scans DoD contractor registrations, identifies companies with active DoD contracts but no CMMC certification, cross-references with hiring activity for compliance roles, and scores each based on contract value and certification deadline proximity. The firm receives 50-80 qualified leads per week — companies that need CMMC consulting and are on a deadline. Conversion rate: 3x their previous manual prospecting.

Scenario 02

A SaaS company targets mid-market companies outgrowing their current tools

The system monitors job postings for titles indicating process maturity (VP of Ops, Director of Revenue Operations), technology stack signals showing competitor product usage, and headcount growth data exceeding 30% YoY. Each lead includes the specific technology gap, estimated deal size, and draft outreach referencing their growth trajectory. Pipeline value increases 140% in the first quarter.

Scenario 03

A consulting firm needs to identify organizations with upcoming ERP transitions

The system scans for SAP/Oracle end-of-life triggers, job postings for ERP implementation roles, RFIs mentioning system modernization, and vendor conference registrations. Each lead is enriched with current system intelligence, estimated project timeline, and decision-maker map. The firm enters conversations six months before the RFP drops.

Scenario 04

A staffing agency monitors contract awards to predict hiring surges

When a government contractor wins a new award, they need to staff it within 30-60 days. The system monitors contract awards in real-time, identifies the labor categories required from the solicitation, and alerts the staffing agency before the contractor even posts the positions. First-mover advantage on every new requirement.

Scenario 05

A managed services provider targets companies after data breach disclosures

The system scans breach notification databases, SEC 8-K filings, and state attorney general disclosure lists. When a company discloses a breach, the system generates a complete lead profile within 24 hours: company size, current security stack, compliance requirements, and recommended approach. Response time matters — the first vendor to reach out after a breach wins 40% of the time.
